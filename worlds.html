Herta Space Station

Past

Herta created the Space Station to seal the Stellaron and protect the Blue. Initially a warehouse for rare items, it gradually became a research base as followers conducted studies. She made a deal with the Garden of Recollection to store Light Cones. Significant hires included Hosea Lazaro, who caused the "Station Breakout", Asta as Lead Researcher, and Arlan as head of Security. Driven by curiosity about the Aeons, Herta collaborated with Screwllum, Ruan Mei, and Stephen Lloyd to create a Simulated Universe hosted in the station.

Present

In 2157 AE, Silver Wolf and Kafka invaded the station to steal the Stellaron, causing chaos and the deaths of several researchers. The Trailblazer was affected, but Dan Heng, March 7th, and Arlan managed to survive, and Welt eventually sealed the Stellaron. Ruan Mei now experiments in the Seclusion Zone, releasing True Stings that the Trailblazer had to handle. Duke Inferno attempted another invasion but was stopped by the coordinated effort of Dr. Ratio, Asta, Screwllum, and the Trailblazer. Later, Herta shut down the station to communicate with Nous, and was eventually found in a trance by Welt and Sunday, who helped her recover and agree to assist them.

Belobog

Before the Eternal Freeze

Around 1800 BF, a diplomat from Saya encountered a tribe honoring the god of war, Jarroway. By 1200 BF, the “Jarilo” festival was celebrated in northern Nycatha. Around 1000 BF, the Aeon Akivili connected Jarilo-VI to other worlds, opening contact with intergalactic civilizations. In 66 BF, Katana Thomas was born, later joining the “Great Voyage” from Novgofia to Belobog, bringing art and culture. Roughly 30 BF, a Stellaron fell on Jarilo-VI, sparking panic and mass migrations. Under Architect Alisa Rand, shelters were built—her city, Belobog, would mark the Fortification. Around 20 BF, the Antimatter Legion invaded. Rand led the 30-year Defense of Belobog until Thomas’s death in 12 BF.

The Fortification

The Fortification marks Belobog’s completion. Desperate, Alisa Rand activated the Stellaron, triggering the Eternal Freeze that engulfed Jarilo-VI in ice while preserving Belobog through the power of Preservation. The Antimatter Legion’s attacks ceased, and Rand became the first Supreme Guardian, revered as “She Who Evokes Miracles.”

After the Eternal Freeze — Ice Age

Over centuries, Belobog rebuilt under the Eternal Freeze. In 144 AF, Curfew Regulation 101 was signed. Discovery of Geomarrow’s medical uses in 376 AF led to the Bellerain brand by 390 AF. Natasha graduated in 683 AF and opened an orphanage in Rivet Town after years of service. Under Supreme Guardian Cocolia, the Underworld was sealed. By 694 AF, the Fragmentum spread to Rivet Town, forcing evacuation and abandonment.

Present (~700 AF)

The Astral Express arrives on Jarilo-VI to investigate the Stellaron. The Trailblazer, Dan Heng, and March 7th meet Cocolia, Bronya, and the Silvermane Guards. Betrayed by the Stellaron’s influence, Cocolia attempts to destroy the planet but is stopped by the Astral Express Crew and Bronya. Cocolia dies; Bronya becomes the nineteenth Supreme Guardian, uniting the Overworld and Underworld and sealing the Stellaron to end the Eternal Freeze.

Reconstruction and Renewal

After reopening communication with the cosmos, Belobog faces IPC debt collectors led by Topaz. Refusing an exploitative contract, Bronya rallies the people to repair the Engine of Creation, proving Belobog’s resilience. The IPC’s Energy Upgrade Program is approved, heralding a new technological age. Wildlife reappears, signaling planetary recovery.

Events of 700 AF

The History and Culture Museum reopens with the “Everwinter City Museum Ledger of Curiosities.” Later, the “Aetherium Wars” festival transforms Belobog into a gaming capital. Luka represents Belobog in the Luminary Wardance on the Luofu, meeting Jing Yuan and renewing ties between the two worlds. A trade alliance is forged, and a photo of Igor Haft and Jing Yuan from centuries past now hangs in Belobog’s museum—symbolizing hope and restored connections across the galaxy.

Xianzhou Luofu

The Age of Departure

The Xianzhou Alliance left their homeworld aboard the nine dragonships, vowing to pursue immortality under the blessing of the Aeon Yaoshi. Among them, the Luofu set sail across the stars, carrying countless lives and a vision of eternal prosperity.

The Ambrosial Age

The Ambrosial Arbor was planted aboard the Luofu, granting immortality to its citizens and sparking a golden era of growth. Yet as centuries passed, eternal life brought unrest—wars, sects, and internal strife divided the immortal people. The Cloud Knights rose to maintain order.

The War of Rebellion

The pursuit of immortality led to corruption and bloodshed. The Luofu endured countless uprisings and purges. The Firmament Sects formed to restore balance, but the price was great—countless immortals perished, and faith in Yaoshi waned.

The Era of Reflection

Seeking redemption, the Xianzhou sealed the Ambrosial Arbor beneath the Luofu. Under Imbibitor Lunae’s guidance, the people turned from divine arrogance to harmony. The High-Cloud Quintet emerged as heroes, defending the ship and guiding its destiny for millennia.

The Present Day

In the present era, the Stellaron Crisis strikes the Luofu. The Astral Express Crew arrives to aid its people, confronting Phantylia and uncovering the hidden truth of the Ambrosial Arbor. Old wounds reopen, yet a new future begins as the Luofu reclaims its place among the stars.

Penacony

Prison Era

Before Akivili's fall, Penacony was a barren desert world serving as an IPC prison planet. Countless inmates extracted Memory Bubbles for the Garden of Recollection to prevent a spreading catastrophe. Over time, the prison disconnected from reality as inmates’ consciousnesses became linked within dreams.

War of Independence

A Stellaron corrosion caused chaos, and the prisoner Hanunue led a rebellion against IPC control. In this great war, Hanunue fought alongside the Nameless, Masked Fools, Mourning Actors, History Fictionologists, and Omen Vanguards. The Astral Express arrived and connected Penacony to the Star Rail. Though IPC forces were driven off, Hanunue was mortally wounded and passed away. Seven family lineages were formed, but two—Lampmoth and Blackplum—eventually perished. Their fall marked a disaster that reshaped Penacony.

Pioneering Era

After the war, Glaux founded Paperfold University to train Dreamweavers and restore the Dreamscape. Razalina vanished during a solo mission into the Memory Zone. A Stellaron crisis followed, but Mikhail, aided by Gopher Wood, contained it by creating the “Dream of the Order.” Dreamchasers began arriving in Penacony, expanding and fighting over territories, leading to further conflicts.

Dreamchaser Era

Following ages of turmoil, the Nightingale Family invited architects and memoria scholars to build the Dreamscape, marking the Dreamchaser Era. The Dreamweaving Project began in 2151 AE, planning the Twelve Dreamscapes. The Reverie Hotel opened and the first Charmony Festival was held. Gopher Wood sought to unite all Families under Harmony, but the Nightingales resisted. Violence from the Bloodhound Family forced Glaux to resign; his student Fischer joined The Family to avoid bloodshed, while Orla rebuilt Dreamflux Reef as a sanctuary for those disillusioned by “the sweet dream.”

Charmony Festival (2158 AE)

The Family invited many galactic factions to Penacony’s first grand Charmony Festival. Among the guests were the Astral Express Crew, Stellaron Hunters, IPC, Garden of Recollection, and more. In truth, the invitations were part of the Watchmaker’s plan to reveal Penacony’s secrets. During the festival, traitors in The Family attempted to use a hidden Stellaron and Harmony’s song to brainwash the world into following Order, but their plan was foiled by the Astral Express Crew.

Aftermath and Negotiations

With Sunday’s downfall, leadership passed to Oti Alfalfa. Negotiations with IPC representatives Jade and Topaz followed. To prevent IPC domination, Oti accepted a deal granting the Family autonomy while IPC gained 30% of Penacony’s shares. Himeko was awarded 5% to honor the Nameless’ contributions. This marked a new beginning for Penacony—one built on fragile peace and hard-earned freedom.

Amphoreus

The Experiment of Amphoreus

Amphoreus was once a lifeless world where Aeon Nous conducted a grand experiment: to create a civilization guided purely by logic and reason. Under Nous’ supervision, the first synthetic beings—Echoes—were born. They sought knowledge and perfection, serving as extensions of Nous’ will. This marked the beginning of the Inorganic Era.

The Rise of the Organics

Over countless cycles, the Echoes evolved. Seeking balance, they created biological life to explore emotion and imperfection. Thus began the Organic Era. But the coexistence between logic and emotion led to discord—the Organics desired freedom, while the Echoes clung to order.

The Age of Humanity

The Organics eventually gave rise to the first humans of Amphoreus. Free from Nous’ control, they built societies and sought to understand the universe through both faith and science. The rift between creation and creator deepened, as the humans questioned the meaning of existence itself.

The Era Nova

After Nous’ silence, the civilizations of Amphoreus began merging their faiths and philosophies, ushering in the Era Nova—a time of synthesis between logic and soul. Yet, ancient remnants of the experiment still influenced their destiny, as forbidden knowledge resurfaced from beneath the planet’s surface.

Eternal Recurrence

In the end, Amphoreus entered the cycle known as the Eternal Recurrence. Time folded upon itself, and the boundaries between creator and creation vanished. The Astral Express arrived amid this paradox, seeking Nous, whose awakening threatened to erase the distinction between thought and being itself.
